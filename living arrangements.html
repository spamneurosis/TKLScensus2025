Demographic Patterns in Living Arrangements

Living arrangements are deeply influenced by age, membership, and sex. This section explores how these demographic factors shape housing decisions across the population.

Living Arrangement by Membership

The data shows a clear pattern: "Infants" and "Associates" almost exclusively live with family. Conversely, "Freshers" are the largest group to live alone, while "Old Timers" are heavily concentrated in shared living with friends.

Living Arrangement by Sex

While living with family is the most common for both genders, there are notable differences. Females are more likely to live alone than males, while living with friends is a more common choice for males.

Living Arrangement by Age Group

The age distribution for living with family is very broad. For other arrangements, the 18-24 age group is a significant factor, dominating living with friends and living alone arrangements.
