A New Look at Living Arrangements

The Age of Transition: Leaving the Nest

This analysis focuses on the journey away from the family home. While living with family is the baseline, a significant shift occurs during young adulthood. This chart tracks the percentage of each age group that lives independently (with friends, alone, or other arrangements).

Who Lives Where? A Demographic Profile

Different living arrangements attract distinct demographic groups. This section compares gender preferences and highlights the dominant membership tiers within each housing type.

Gender Profiles in Housing

This radar chart compares the living arrangement choices for males and females. It highlights that females have a stronger tendency to live alone, while males are more inclined to live with friends.

Membership Hotspots

This chart reveals which membership tier is most dominant within each living situation. "Associates" form the largest group living with family, while "Old Timers" are the biggest cohort living with friends.
