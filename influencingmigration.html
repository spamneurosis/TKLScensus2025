Sources of Influence

Beyond the primary reasons, it is also important to understand who or what influenced the decision to migrate. The data reveals that personal and family influence are the most significant drivers.

Influence by Membership

While "Parents/Family" are a major influence for all groups, "Freshers" are heavily influenced by "Self," indicating a strong sense of personal agency. "Old Timers" are also influenced by "Friends/Peers" and "Church/Religious" institutions.

Influence by Sex

Both genders are influenced by "Parents/Family" and "Self" in roughly equal proportions. Males, however, show a slightly higher proportion of influence from "Friends/Peers," while females are more influenced by "Church/Religious" institutions.

Influence by Age Group

Influence is highly dependent on age. Younger age groups (0-17) are almost entirely influenced by "Parents/Family," while older groups (18-24) show a dramatic increase in self-influence.

Influence Trends Over Time

The influence of "Self" has increased dramatically in recent years, particularly since 2022, while the influence of "Parents/Family" has remained relatively constant. This suggests a shift toward more independent decision-making among recent migrants.
